Open Concept: Versatile Accessible Elevated Housing Model
Core Idea
A flexible, low-impact housing design for 4-bedroom homes that can be used in many ways: permanent family homes, small residential estates, holiday lets / Airbnbs, or clustered hamlets with optional health support. The design is future-proof and adaptable from day one, so it can easily serve different needs over time.
The Homes
Elevated on helical (screw) piles to minimise ground disturbance and concrete use.
Flexible pile placement so the structure can work around trees, roots, wildlife habitats, and underground services.
Triangulated structural platform for strength and modularity.
Designed as a basic adaptable shell:
Hoist-ready walls and ceilings in all bedrooms (hidden reinforcement points).
Proper circulation space, level access, and wide doors throughout.
One fully accessible bathroom + one family bathroom.
Height-adjustable kitchen worktops as standard, so wheelchair users and standing family members can prepare meals together comfortably.
Open-plan kitchen/lounge area.
Permanent integrated ramp and perimeter deck for easy outdoor access.
Can be built with modular/factory components and easily adapted later based on changing needs (via OT assessment, standard upgrades, or simple refits).
Possible Uses
Permanent family homes or small estates (8–20 homes).
Holiday lets / Airbnbs — attractive, accessible accommodation that appeals to a wider range of guests.
Community hamlets with optional on-site health hub (OTs, district nurses) to support hospital discharge and family care.
Other flexible applications where low-impact, adaptable housing is valuable.
Purpose
Create genuinely accessible and future-proof homes (far beyond minimum standards of wide doors and a downstairs toilet).
Support low-impact development on varied or sensitive sites.
Offer versatility: the same core design works for everyday living, holiday use, or specialised care settings.
Reduce future retrofit costs — the home is already prepared for changing accessibility needs.
This concept is offered as a free, open idea for anyone to use, modify, or develop further. It is not a complete technical specification — any real build requires professional structural engineering, site surveys, planning permission, and compliance with Building Regulations.
