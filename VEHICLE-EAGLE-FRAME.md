# AUTO FREE: THE MODULAR CARGO VEHICLE (EAGLE FRAMEWORK)
### Supplemental Blueprint: Utility Spacecraft for Interplanetary Resource Logistics

> **The Vision:** To support the Mass Launcher and the United Aerospace network, we require a rugged, adaptable, and completely unweaponized transport vehicle. Inspired by classic utilitarian engineering—like the modular *Space: 1999* Eagle framework—this craft is designed to land on unpredictable terrain, swap payloads instantly, and utilize zero-gravity 3D printing to scale logistics exponentially.

---

## 1. The Modular Airframe & Capsule System

True versatility means the vehicle's core structure never changes, but its function can be swapped out in minutes.

* **The Open-Truss Spine:** A high-strength, structural backbone that carries the cockpit, engines, and landing gear.
* **Interchangeable Cargo Pods:** The central fuselage is a completely removable capsule. It can be dropped off and replaced seamlessly—one flight it is a specialized medical module to support a Care Hamlet, the next it is a heavy-duty container filled with processed desert silicon or deep-drilled elements.
* **Independent Landing Array:** Heavy-duty, articulating landing legs with wide, shock-absorbing footpads, allowing the craft to touch down safely on unpaved planetary surfaces, rocky terrain, or desert staging grounds.

---

## 2. Propulsion & Atmospheric Control

Designed for absolute pilot control during vertical takeoffs, landings, and planetary re-entry.

* **Pivoting Thruster Jets:** Side-mounted maneuver thrusters that physically rotate up and down. This allows the ship to convert seamlessly between vertical takeoff/landing (VTOL) and forward planetary transit.
* **Mechanical Iris Shields:** Heavy-duty, interlocking metal iris shutters that close completely over the thruster bells and sensitive navigation sensors during atmospheric re-entry or high-density debris transits, protecting the ship's core systems from thermal damage.

---

## 3. Zero-Gravity 3D Printing (The 10x Velocity Multiplier)

To rapidly scale the Auto Free network across the solar system, these transport ships carry automated, industrial 3D printing units.

* **Self-Replication Limits:** The onboard printers are pre-programmed to manufacture replacement parts and even replicate their own printing components to scale up operations. However, they are strictly governed and hard-coded with finite limits to prevent over-replication.
* **Exponential Construction Speed:** By fabricating structural frames, landing pads, and habitat extensions directly in space using local resources, construction moves 10 times quicker—allowing the network to deploy thousands of units rapidly without waiting for supplies from Earth.
